## E. Skill Category Interpretation

### Number Sense and Place Value

- 0-40%: Student needs support with counting, comparing numbers, and place value.
- 41-70%: Student has partial place value understanding but still makes errors.
- 71-100%: Student is secure and ready for larger numbers and more advanced operations.

### Addition and Subtraction

- 0-40%: Student needs support with basic facts and simple addition/subtraction.
- 41-70%: Student can solve some sums but may make regrouping or place value errors.
- 71-100%: Student is secure with core addition and subtraction skills.

### Multiplication and Division Readiness

- 0-40%: Student needs concrete work on equal groups, sharing, and repeated addition.
- 41-70%: Student understands the ideas but may be weak in fact recall.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for stronger multiplication and division work.

### Fractions

- 0-40%: Student needs support with fractions as equal parts of a whole.
- 41-70%: Student understands some fraction ideas but may confuse comparison.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for broader fraction work.

### Money and Time

- 0-40%: Student needs support with coins, money notation, and reading time.
- 41-70%: Student can solve familiar tasks but may struggle with change or elapsed time.
- 71-100%: Student is secure with practical money and time tasks.

### Measurement

- 0-40%: Student needs support with units and measuring ideas.
- 41-70%: Student shows some understanding but may make conversion or perimeter errors.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for more advanced measurement work.

### Geometry and Shapes

- 0-40%: Student needs support naming and recognising shapes.
- 41-70%: Student recognises some shapes but may be unsure about properties.
- 71-100%: Student is secure with basic geometry recognition.

### Data and Graphs

- 0-40%: Student needs support reading simple picture graphs.
- 41-70%: Student can read some graph information but may miss scale.
- 71-100%: Student reads simple data displays accurately.

### Word Problems and Mathematical Reasoning

- 0-40%: Student needs support choosing the correct operations in context.
- 41-70%: Student can solve some routine problems but may lose track in multi-step tasks.
- 71-100%: Student applies operations appropriately in context.

## F. Recommended Grouping Plan

| Group | Suggested Profile | Teaching Focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Group 1: Foundations | Students with very low overall scores or weak number and operation basics | Counting, number order, number bonds, simple addition/subtraction, shape recognition. |
| Group 2: Lower Primary Skills | Students with partial basic skills but clear gaps in place value, money, time, and early multiplication | Place value, addition/subtraction fluency, money, time, graph reading, multiplication readiness. |
| Group 3: Middle Primary Skills | Students mostly secure with basics and ready for fractions, measurement, and word problems | Multiplication/division, fractions, perimeter, money, time, applied problem solving. |
| Group 4: Extension Group | Students with very high scores and mostly secure understanding across strands | Harder word problems, stronger fraction work, area/perimeter, and richer reasoning tasks. |

## G. Short Teacher Notes on How to Use the Pre-Test

1. This shorter version works well as a 20-25 minute screen or quick placement check.
2. Mark by category as well as by total score.
3. If a student does poorly overall, follow up with oral counting, number recognition, and concrete materials.
4. This revised short version has a stronger middle and upper range than the first draft, but the 40-question version still gives finer placement detail.
