## E. Skill Category Interpretation

### Number Sense and Place Value

- 0-40%: Student needs support with counting, comparing numbers, number order, and basic place value.
- 41-70%: Student shows partial place value understanding but may make mistakes with larger numbers or rounding.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for larger numbers, stronger place value work, and more advanced operations.

### Addition and Subtraction

- 0-40%: Student needs support with basic facts, number bonds, and simple addition and subtraction.
- 41-70%: Student can solve some number sentences but may make regrouping or place value errors.
- 71-100%: Student is secure with core addition and subtraction skills and is ready for more complex calculation.

### Multiplication and Division Readiness

- 0-40%: Student needs concrete practice with equal groups, repeated addition, sharing, and basic facts.
- 41-70%: Student understands the ideas but may be slow or inaccurate with facts and division.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for stronger multiplication/division fluency and harder problem solving.

### Fractions

- 0-40%: Student needs support with fractions as equal parts of a whole and simple fraction language.
- 41-70%: Student understands some fraction ideas but may confuse comparison, equivalence, or simplification.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for broader fraction work, including mixed numbers and fraction operations.

### Money and Time

- 0-40%: Student needs support with counting money, reading clocks, and understanding hour and minute ideas.
- 41-70%: Student can manage familiar money and time tasks but may struggle with change, duration, or 24-hour time.
- 71-100%: Student is secure with practical money and time skills and is ready for more complex applications.

### Measurement

- 0-40%: Student needs support choosing units, comparing measures, and understanding length or area language.
- 41-70%: Student shows some measurement understanding but may make errors with conversion, perimeter, or area.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for more advanced measurement tasks and multi-step area/perimeter work.

### Geometry and Shapes

- 0-40%: Student needs support with naming shapes, recognising properties, and basic spatial language.
- 41-70%: Student recognises common shapes and lines but may be unsure about symmetry or shape properties.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for more advanced geometry, including angles, symmetry, and shape analysis.

### Data and Graphs

- 0-40%: Student needs support reading simple graphs or tables and matching data to pictures.
- 41-70%: Student can read some data displays but may miss scale, totals, or comparison details.
- 71-100%: Student is ready to interpret data displays more confidently and explain results.

### Word Problems and Mathematical Reasoning

- 0-40%: Student needs support choosing operations, understanding key information, and explaining thinking.
- 41-70%: Student can solve some routine word problems but may struggle with multi-step reasoning.
- 71-100%: Student is ready for more complex contextual problems and can apply skills flexibly.

## F. Recommended Grouping Plan

| Group | Suggested Profile | Teaching Focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Group 1: Foundations | Students with very low overall scores or weak Number Sense and Addition/Subtraction results | Counting, number order, comparing quantities, number bonds, simple addition/subtraction, basic shapes. |
| Group 2: Lower Primary Skills | Students who know basic numbers but still show gaps in place value, money, time, and early multiplication | Place value, addition/subtraction fluency, money, time, simple multiplication and division, graph reading. |
| Group 3: Middle Primary Skills | Students who are mostly secure with basic operations and are ready for fractions, measurement, and more structured problem solving | Multiplication/division fluency, fractions, perimeter/area, time duration, money, word problems. |
| Group 4: Extension Group | Students with high total scores and strong category scores across most strands | Multi-step reasoning, fraction of a set, advanced area/perimeter, richer data tasks, and challenge problems. |

### Quick placement rule

- Place in Group 1 if total score is 0-10, or if Number Sense and Addition/Subtraction are both below 50%.
- Place in Group 2 if total score is 11-20, or if basics are secure but Money and Time, Multiplication/Division, or Measurement are weak.
- Place in Group 3 if total score is 21-30, especially when the student is ready for fractions and longer word problems.
- Place in Group 4 if total score is 31-40 and the student also scores at least 70% in most categories.

## G. Short Teacher Notes on How to Use the Pre-Test

1. Give the test in one sitting if possible. About 40-50 minutes is suitable for most students. Younger students may need a little more time.
2. Let students attempt the paper independently first. For very young or hesitant students, you may read questions aloud without helping with the mathematics.
3. Mark by category, not just by total score. A student with a moderate total may still have a major gap in one strand.
4. Use the category profile to plan instruction groups. Re-teach the weakest strands first, especially Number Sense, Addition/Subtraction, and Multiplication/Division readiness.
5. Keep students' written work. Error patterns are useful for follow-up interviews or short oral checks.
6. Reuse a small set of similar questions after intervention to measure growth.
